Real signal, not guesses

How Problem Solver works

Problem Solver is where you bring an idea, a product, or a decision and get real input from people, not a stranger's five second reaction. You share as much or as little as you want, then bring in the people whose opinion would actually matter.

For example: say you're thinking about launching an avocado based skincare mask before you spend any money building it. You post the idea on Diskorse: what you're picturing, what price point you're considering, what you're unsure about. From there you can invite people who are into skincare, or who know the space, to actually talk it through with you, and you can put it out as a poll for the wider community to react to as well.
Anyone can browse open Problem Solvers for free. Posting your own, or applying to contribute to someone else's, needs the Engage plan ($5/mo). See Pricing & Fees.

The technical bit

Pricing & how it works

Every question we'd expect a founder or contributor to actually ask.

Do I need to pay to use Problem Solver?+

You can browse open Problem Solvers for free. To post your own, or to apply and contribute to someone else's, you'll need the Engage plan ($5/mo). Paying a specific collaborator on top of that is a separate, per conversation cost you set yourself.

Which plan do I need for this?+

Engage ($5/mo) unlocks full Problem Solver access, posting and contributing both. See Pricing & Fees for the full plan breakdown.

How does paid collaboration actually work?+

When you accept someone, Diskorse holds your payment in escrow. It's released to them once your conversation is marked complete, so nobody is paying upfront for a conversation that might not happen.

Can I set a different price for each collaborator?+

Yes. You're not locked into one flat rate. Someone with deep, relevant expertise might be worth more to you than a general reaction, and you can price it that way.

How do I make sure I'm actually getting useful input?+

You're never charged until you accept someone. Before accepting, you can review their profile, their Vouches, and what they said about why they're a good fit, so you're choosing, not gambling.

Can I invite someone who isn't on Diskorse yet?+

Yes. You can invite people from outside Diskorse. They'll need to join to accept the invite, have the conversation, and get paid.

Is a Problem Solver conversation private?+

Yes, each accepted conversation is private between you and that person. Polls, on the other hand, are public, since the point there is broad, visible reaction.
